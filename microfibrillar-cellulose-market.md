<p><p>The microfibrillar cellulose market is categorized into three main types based on production methods. The acid hydrolysis method uses acids to selectively break down cellulose fibers, enhancing their properties for various applications. The enzymatic hydrolysis method employs enzymes to achieve a more controlled and environmentally friendly breakdown of cellulose, resulting in high-quality microfibrils. The electrospinning technique involves using an electric charge to produce nanofibers from cellulose solutions, creating fine, uniform fibers suitable for advanced applications like filtration and tissue engineering.</p></p>
